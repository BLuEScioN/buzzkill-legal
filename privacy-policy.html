BuzzKill Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 23, 2026 Last updated: July 23, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Altair Labs, LLC ("BuzzKill," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, shares, and protects information when you use the BuzzKill mobile application and related services (the "App"). By using the App you agree to this Policy. If you do not agree, do not use the App.

BuzzKill is a sobriety and alcohol-reduction support app. Because of what it does, we handle information that may be sensitive, including data about your drinking and recovery. We describe below exactly what we collect and why.

Not medical care. BuzzKill is a motivational, educational, and accountability tool. It is not medical care, therapy, or a substitute for professional help, and it is not for emergencies. See our Terms of Service and the in-app safety information. If you are in crisis, call or text 988 (in the U.S.) or your local emergency number.

1. Who we are (data controller)

Altair Labs, LLC, 5900 Balcones Dr, Ste 100, Austin, TX 78731, is the controller responsible for your personal information. Contact: buzzkilliosapp@gmail.com. If you are in the EU or UK, you may contact us about your personal information at buzzkilliosapp@gmail.com.

2. Information we collect

  • Account and profile. When you create an account with email/password or sign in with Apple or Google, we receive your name (if provided) and email address, or the identifier those providers return. If you use Sign in with Apple and choose to hide your email, we receive Apple's private relay address. We also receive any profile photo you add.
  • Recovery and health information you choose to log. Because BuzzKill helps you track sobriety, much of what you enter concerns your health. This includes your quit date; daily check-ins marking days as sober or as a setback; details you add about setbacks (such as reasons, drink types, quantities, and estimated costs); mood and craving ratings; your onboarding assessment answers; and the progress photos you add to the selfie tracker. We treat this as sensitive information.
  • Content you create. Journal entries, check-in reflections, affirmations, and anything you choose to post in community features.
  • Messages to our AI features. What you send to the in-app AI companion ("Lex") and the context we assemble to answer you (see Section 6).
  • Communications. Messages you send us for support.

2.2 Health data (Apple HealthKit)

If you connect Apple Health, the App reads the following from Apple HealthKit to show you personal health statistics: step count, active energy burned (calories), resting heart rate, and sleep analysis. You control this permission in iOS Settings and can revoke it at any time. This HealthKit data is displayed to you within the App and stays on your device — we do not transmit it to our servers or to any third party — and it is handled in accordance with Section 10 (HealthKit commitments).

2.3 Information collected automatically

  • Usage and analytics data. App interactions, features used, screens viewed, and events, collected via Mixpanel to understand and improve the App. In Mixpanel we analyze this against a pseudonymous identifier, not your name or email, which we keep first-party in our own database.
  • Device and diagnostic data. Device model, operating-system version, app version, language, time zone, and similar technical information. Crash and performance diagnostics are collected via Firebase Crashlytics.
  • Push notification token. A Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) token so we can send you reminders and notifications (which you can disable in device settings).
  • Advertising and attribution data (only with your permission). If you grant permission through Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt, we and the Meta (Facebook) SDK collect your device's advertising identifier (IDFA), advertising and click identifiers, and standard app events (such as installs, sessions, registrations, and purchases) to measure the effectiveness of our advertising and attribute installs. We do not send Meta the health-related content you log. See Section 5A (Tracking and advertising).

If you do not grant ATT permission, we do not collect your IDFA or send app events to Meta for advertising, and the advertising SDK stays dormant.

2.4 Purchase data

Subscriptions are processed by Apple through StoreKit / the App Store. Apple handles your payment information; we do not receive your credit-card or payment- card details. We receive subscription status, product identifiers, and transaction identifiers needed to grant and manage your subscription and in-app tokens.

3. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • provide the App's core features (streak tracking, check-ins, catch-up, calendar, stats, journal, affirmations, community, and the AI companion);
  • generate personalized content such as weekly progress reports and AI responses;
  • send reminders, milestone messages, and other notifications you enable;
  • process subscriptions and manage the in-app token balance;
  • analyze usage to maintain, secure, debug, and improve the App;
  • communicate with you about support, updates, and changes; and
  • comply with law and enforce our Terms.

4. Legal bases for processing (EEA/UK users)

Where the GDPR/UK GDPR applies, we rely on: performance of a contract (to provide the App you request); consent (for HealthKit access, push notifications, optional sensitive data you choose to log, advertising/attribution tracking via the ATT prompt, and certain analytics — you may withdraw consent at any time); legitimate interests (to secure and improve the App, where not overridden by your rights); and legal obligation. Some information you log concerns health and may be a special category of data; where required, we process it based on your explicit consent.

5. How we share information

We do not sell your personal information for money. We do not share your information for cross-context behavioral advertising except the limited advertising-attribution data described in Section 5A, and only if you grant App Tracking Transparency permission. We share information as follows:

  • Service providers (processors) who act on our instructions:
    • Google / Firebase (Authentication, Firestore database, Cloud Storage, Cloud Functions, Cloud Messaging, in-app messaging, and Crashlytics crash reporting) — hosting and core backend. (Google Analytics is not enabled in the App.)
    • Mixpanel — product analytics (keyed to a pseudonymous identifier).
    • OpenAI — powers the AI companion, journal summaries, and weekly reports; see Section 6.
    • Apple — authentication (Sign in with Apple), HealthKit, and payment processing.
    • Meta Platforms — advertising measurement and install attribution, only if you grant ATT permission (see Section 5A).
  • At your direction, such as content you choose to post in community features.
  • Legal and safety — to comply with law, enforce our Terms, or protect the rights, safety, and property of BuzzKill, our users, or the public.
  • Business transfers — in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

5A. Tracking and advertising (App Tracking Transparency)

We use the Meta (Facebook) SDK to measure our advertising and attribute app installs. This is off by default. Where relevant, iOS shows you Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt asking whether to allow tracking:

  • If you tap Allow, we enable the Meta SDK to collect your advertising identifier (IDFA) and standard app events (installs, sessions, purchases) for advertising attribution and measurement.
  • If you tap Ask App Not to Track, or take no action, the SDK stays dormant and none of that data is collected or shared for advertising.

We send Meta only standard app events and identifiers for attribution — never the health-related content you log (your check-ins, setback details, mood, cravings, journal, photos, or AI chats). You can change your choice at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking. Because we support ATT, our App Store privacy label lists data "Used to Track You."

6. AI features and your content

The App's AI companion, journal summaries, and weekly reports are generated using OpenAI's API. To produce a response, we send OpenAI the relevant content and context — for example, your recent messages, check-in trends, and journal summaries. OpenAI does not use data submitted through its API to train or improve its models, and we do not permit our AI provider to use your content for that purpose. OpenAI may retain API content for a limited period (up to 30 days) to monitor for abuse, after which it is deleted, unless a longer period is required by law. Please avoid entering information in AI features that you would not want processed for this purpose.

7. Data retention

We keep your information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the App, then delete or de-identify it within a reasonable period, unless a longer retention is required by law or for legitimate business purposes (e.g., security, dispute resolution). You can delete your account in the App, which removes your associated data as described in Section 8.

8. Your choices and rights

  • Access, correction, deletion. You can view and edit much of your data in the App, and delete your account (which deletes your associated records).
  • HealthKit and notifications. Manage HealthKit access and push notifications in iOS Settings at any time.
  • EEA/UK rights. Subject to conditions, you may request access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection, and may withdraw consent or lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority.
  • California (CCPA/CPRA) rights. You may request to know, access, correct, and delete personal information, and to limit use of sensitive personal information. We do not sell your personal information for money. If you grant App Tracking Transparency permission, our sharing of advertising identifiers and app events with Meta for advertising attribution may be considered "sharing" (cross-context behavioral advertising) under California law; you can opt out at any time by declining or turning off tracking in iOS Settings (see Section 5A). We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.
  • Other U.S. states. Residents of states with comprehensive privacy laws may have similar rights, subject to the conditions and exceptions in those laws.

To exercise any right, contact buzzkilliosapp@gmail.com. We will verify your request as required by law.

9. Children's privacy

The App is not directed to minors, and you must be at least 18 years old to use it. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If you believe a person under 18 has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

10. Apple HealthKit commitments

Consistent with Apple's requirements, we will not use HealthKit data for advertising or marketing, will not sell it, and will not share it with third parties for advertising, marketing, or data-mining. HealthKit data is used solely to provide the health-statistics features you request and is not disclosed except with your consent or as required by law.

11. Security
